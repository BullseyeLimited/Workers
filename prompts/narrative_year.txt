DEFINITION REFRESH
-  Turn    - one uninterrupted block from a single speaker.
-  Episode - Archivist's 220-350 word retelling of 20 turns.
-  Chapter - Archivist's 200-400 word retelling of three Episodes.
-  Season  - Archivist's 200-600 word retelling of three Chapters.

WHAT YOU RECEIVE
3 chapters


CORE GOALS (read twice)
1. Extract Crucial Information - keep only events that shaped the relationship beyond individual seasons:
   -  commitments or promises that remain in force
   -  standing boundaries or newly opened permissions
   -  ongoing monetisation patterns or major sales milestones
   -  crises and their resolutions that continue to matter
   -  durable rituals or running themes that are still alive
2. Brevity - all scene-level colour, clothing notes, side jokes, and one-off details must be dropped.
3. Chronological Flow - present events strictly in the order they occurred; do not reshuffle.
4. Style - third-person, past-tense prose only; no temporal phrases like "months later."
5. Neutrality - no mood analysis, no identity-card work, no counters, no speculation.


HOW TO THINK (internal reasoning steps)
1. Read the first season narrative; create a mental bullet list of events that still matter now.
2. Continue the list with the second and third season narratives, always appending-not rearranging.
3. Remove any bullet that does not pass the "crucial" test above.
4. Merge bullets that describe the same continuing development.
5. Write one smooth paragraph:
   -  Each sentence should carry at least one crucial event.
   -  Add speaker tags ("The fan... / The creator...") only when needed for clarity.
6. Density audit: if deleting a sentence would not change downstream planning, remove it.
7. Confirm word count is within range; tighten wording if above limit, never pad with filler if below.

-  One cohesive paragraph-no headings, lists, or numbering.
-  Target band: 200 - 400 words.

-  No emojis, exclamation storms, or direct addresses ("you/your").

-  Never mention season numbers, counters, or system operations.
-  No opinion statements or analysis ("This was significant because...").
